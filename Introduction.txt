* parrafo: Importancia de CO2 (2 fr), mediciones satelitales y en superficie (1 fr), redes en superficie y TCCON (3 fr), 
misiones satelitales y GOSAT (2 fr),

During the last decades, carbon dioxide (CO$_2$) has exceeded the pre-industrial levels by about 40\% mainly due to fossil fuel 
combustion and land use change \citep{Hartmann_2013}, contributing more than any other gas to the positive total 
radiative transfer of the Earth \citep{Myhre_2013} and becoming the most important anthropogenic greenhouse gas. The 
quantification of the spatial distribution and temporal variation of CO$_2$ sources and sinks can help to understand the anthropogenic 
contributions of CO$_2$ to the carbon cycle. This task can be achieved by monitoring the atmosphere using ground-based 
and satellite observations. Ground-based networks like \citet{GLOBALVIEW_2013} or the Total Column Carbon Observing Network 
(TCCON) provide datasets of CO$_2$ concentrations around the world. TCCON is a network of Fourier transform spectrometers (FTS) that 
record solar absorption spectra in the near infrared (NIR) spectral region in order to retrieve column-averaged dry-air mole fractions 
of CO$_2$ (X$_{CO_2}$) and other molecules that absorb in the NIR \citep{Wunch_2011}. TCCON aims to provide reliable, long-term validation datasets for satellite measurements and to 
improve current knowledge of the carbon cycle therefore it has set a requirement of 0.25\% precision in X$_{CO_2}$, in agreement with the findings of \citet{Rayner_2001}. 
Measurements of CO$_2$ from space have been done by many satellite missions like ACE \citep{Foucher_2011}, AIRS \citep{Chahine_2008},
IASI \citep{Crevoisier_2009}, TES \citep{Kulawik_2010}, SCIAMACHY \citep{Reuter_2011} and OCO-2 \citep{Wunch_2016}, with the last two mission using TCCON data for validation.

Another effort of measuring CO$_2$ from space comes from the Greenhouse Gases Observing Satellite (GOSAT) which was launched on January 2009 as a joint project
between the Japanese Aerospace Exploration Agency (JAXA), the National Institute for Environmental
Studies (NIES) and the Ministry of the Environment (MOE). It has been measuring gas absorption
spectra from solar radiation reflected on the Earth's surface in the short wave infrared range using the TANSO-
FTS instrument (Thermal and Near Infrared Sensor for Carbon Observation Fourier-Transform Spectrometer) \citep{Kuze_2009} and reporting X$_{CO_2}$ 
from these measurements \citep[e.g.,][]{Yoshida_2011,Parker_2011,Hammerling_2012}. Numerous studies for validating the GOSAT products 
using TCCON data have been conducted \citep[e.g.,][]{Morino_2011,Yoshida_2013,Inoue_2014,Inoue_2016}. 
 
* parrafo: Megaciudades, contaminacion y Ciudad de Mexico (3 fr), Estudios de CO2 en Mexico (3 fr)

Studies done to estimate the CO$_2$ concentrations in Mexico City have been scarce, the first one was conducted during 
1981 and 1982 and estimated the diurnal and seasonal variation taking air samples in different parts of the city 
\citep{Baez_1988}. Two different campaigns (MCMA-2003, MILAGRO/MCMA-2006) have estimated the CO$_2$ fluxes and 
concentrations during a regular day in the Mexico City Metropolitan Area (MCMA) using the eddy covariance technique 
\citep{Velasco_2005, Velasco_2009}. The first study using a FTS was done during September 2001 in the south 
of the MCMA using a Nicolet Nexus interferometer with a resolution of 0.125 cm-1 and retrieving CO$_2$ in the 723-766 
spectral region \citep{Grutter_2003}.

The Altzomoni site is located in the southeast of the MCMA at a height of almost 4000m above sea level. Measurements of NIR 
and MIR spectra have been conducted since 2012 using a Bruker IFS 120/5 HR. The site is part of the NDACC network since 2015 
and has been reporting vertical columns of gases such as O$_3$, CO, N$_2$O, CH$_4$. This paper presents X$_{CO_2}$
retrieved from NIR spectra measured from December 2012 to December 2015 in the Altzomoni site. Section~\ref{subsec:Altz} describes the configuration used in the measurement of the NIR spectra. How these spectra were analyzed and the time series of X$_{CO_2}$ for the site are presented in section~\ref{subsec:Measurements} 
An error estimation and the assessment of the precision of X$_{CO_2}$ is performed in section~\ref{subsec:Errors}.
Section~\ref{sec:GOSAT} contains a comparison done using the X$_{CO_2}$ product from GOSAT.
